Boys and Girls Club Shelving

Project Overview

As we begin our partnership with the local Boys and Girls Club, this initiative focuses on designing a space-efficient shelving system for their storage room. The team will collaborate closely with club members to ensure the final design reflects day-to-day needs and long-term usability.

Kick-Off Objectives

  • Conduct site visits to document existing storage conditions and spatial constraints
  • Gather detailed requirements through conversations with Boys and Girls Club staff and members
  • Create initial sketches and alternative shelving concepts tailored to the space

Design Collaboration

Throughout the semester the group will iterate on shelving layouts, incorporating direct feedback from the club. Each revision emphasizes accessibility, durability, and efficient use of vertical and horizontal space.

Feasibility Analysis

  • Evaluate the material options that balance cost, availability, and ease of assembly
  • Assess structural stability and load considerations for frequently used storage items
  • Plan for modular elements that allow future expansion or reconfiguration

Implementation Path

After finalizing a pitch-ready design, the team will outline construction logistics, from procurement to build sequencing. During installation, EWB students will act as facilitators—supporting club members and volunteers as they bring the design to life.

Get Involved

Students interested in community engagement, human-centered design, and hands-on fabrication are encouraged to join. Help shape a project that blends technical planning with collaborative construction and delivers an immediate benefit to our local partners.
