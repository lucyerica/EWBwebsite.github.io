Lehigh Valley Water Quality Sampling

Project Overview

The Lehigh Valley Water Quality Sampling project involves systematic collection and analysis of water samples from various sources throughout the Lehigh Valley to monitor environmental health and water resource quality.

Sampling Objectives

  • Monitor water quality in local rivers, streams, and water bodies
  • Assess the impact of urban development on water resources
  • Track seasonal variations in water quality parameters
  • Identify potential pollution sources and contamination
  • Support environmental protection and conservation efforts

Sampling Locations

  • Lehigh River and its tributaries
  • Local streams and creeks
  • Stormwater retention ponds
  • Municipal water treatment facilities
  • Industrial and residential discharge points

Parameters Tested

  • pH and dissolved oxygen levels
  • Turbidity and suspended solids
  • Nutrient content (nitrogen, phosphorus)
  • Heavy metals and chemical contaminants
  • Bacterial indicators and pathogens
  • Temperature and conductivity

Methodology

Our team follows standardized EPA protocols for water sampling and analysis, ensuring data quality and comparability. We use both field testing equipment and laboratory analysis to obtain comprehensive results.

Data Management

  • Digital data collection and storage systems
  • Quality assurance and quality control procedures
  • Statistical analysis and trend identification
  • Public reporting and data sharing

Community Partnerships

We collaborate with local environmental organizations, government agencies, and citizen science groups to maximize the impact and utility of our water quality monitoring efforts.

Educational Component

Students gain hands-on experience in environmental monitoring, analytical chemistry, and data analysis while contributing to important environmental research and community health protection.

Impact and Applications

The data collected supports local environmental decision-making, helps identify emerging water quality issues, and contributes to broader regional environmental monitoring efforts.

Join Our Team

Students interested in environmental engineering, analytical chemistry, field research, and environmental protection are encouraged to participate in this important community service project.
