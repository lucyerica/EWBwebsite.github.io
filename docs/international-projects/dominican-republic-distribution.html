Dominican Republic Distribution

Project Overview

Community members of La Recta de Sanita often experience water insecurity with their current, government controlled water system. To ensure that La Recta de Sanita can achieve water independence, our chapter is continuing previous semesters work in developing a detailed map system (with pumps, pipes, valves, etc.) that will be implemented across La Recta de Sanita.

Community Background

La Recta de Sanita is a rural community in the Dominican Republic that faces significant challenges with water access and reliability. The existing infrastructure is inadequate to meet the community's growing needs and often fails to provide consistent water supply.

Current Challenges

  • Inconsistent water supply from government-controlled systems
  • Inadequate water pressure in residential areas
  • Aging and unreliable distribution infrastructure
  • Limited access to clean water during dry seasons
  • High dependency on external water sources

Project Objectives

  • Design a comprehensive water distribution network
  • Develop detailed mapping of pipe systems and components
  • Calculate optimal flow rates and pressure requirements
  • Ensure equitable water access across the community
  • Create a sustainable and maintainable system

Technical Approach

The distribution group is modeling water system features such as the lengths of pipes, flow rates, pressures and losses. Our team uses hydraulic modeling software and engineering calculations to optimize the distribution network design.

Key Components

  • Main distribution lines and secondary networks
  • Pumping stations and pressure regulation systems
  • Storage tanks and distribution points
  • Control valves and flow measurement devices
  • Service connections to individual households

System Design Considerations

  • Population growth and future water demand
  • Topographical challenges and elevation changes
  • Local materials availability and cost
  • Community capacity for operation and maintenance
  • Integration with existing infrastructure

Community Impact

This distribution system will provide reliable water access to hundreds of community members, improving health outcomes, economic opportunities, and overall quality of life. The project emphasizes community ownership and long-term sustainability.

Student Learning Outcomes

Students working on this project gain experience in hydraulic design, community engagement, international development, and sustainable engineering practices while making a meaningful impact on a global community.

Get Involved

Students interested in hydraulic engineering, international development, CAD design, and community service are encouraged to join this impactful project that directly improves lives in the Dominican Republic.
