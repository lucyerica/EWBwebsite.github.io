Dominican Republic Storage Group

Project Overview

The Dominican Republic Storage Group focuses on designing and implementing water storage solutions for the community of La Recta de Sanita, ensuring reliable water availability during periods of supply interruption and supporting overall water security.

Storage Challenges

La Recta de Sanita faces significant challenges with water storage and reliability. The community requires adequate storage capacity to maintain water security during supply interruptions and peak demand periods.

Key Storage Issues

  • Insufficient storage capacity for community needs
  • Lack of reliable backup water sources
  • Seasonal variations in water availability
  • Inadequate distribution pressure during peak times
  • Limited redundancy in the water supply system

Project Objectives

  • Design appropriate water storage systems
  • Calculate optimal storage capacity requirements
  • Ensure water quality preservation during storage
  • Integrate storage with treatment and distribution systems
  • Develop sustainable maintenance protocols

Storage Solutions

Our team evaluates various storage technologies and designs systems that meet community needs while considering local conditions, materials availability, and long-term sustainability.

Storage System Types

  • Elevated water tanks and towers
  • Ground-level storage reservoirs
  • Underground cistern systems
  • Pressure tanks and pneumatic systems
  • Gravity-fed storage solutions

Design Considerations

  • Community water demand analysis and projections
  • Topographical conditions and elevation requirements
  • Material selection and local availability
  • Construction feasibility and community capacity
  • Integration with existing infrastructure

Capacity Calculations

Our team conducts detailed analyses to determine appropriate storage capacity based on:

  • Daily water consumption patterns
  • Peak demand requirements
  • Emergency reserve needs
  • Seasonal supply variations
  • Future growth projections

Water Quality Preservation

  • Tank design to prevent contamination
  • Proper inlet and outlet configurations
  • Ventilation and overflow systems
  • Regular cleaning and maintenance protocols
  • Water quality monitoring and testing

System Integration

The storage system is designed to work seamlessly with the water treatment and distribution components, ensuring optimal system performance and reliability.

Community Training

We develop comprehensive training programs to build local capacity for storage system operation, maintenance, and quality control, ensuring long-term sustainability.

Impact and Benefits

  • Improved water security and reliability
  • Better pressure and flow in distribution system
  • Reduced dependency on external water sources
  • Enhanced emergency preparedness
  • Support for community growth and development

Student Learning

Students gain practical experience in hydraulic design, structural engineering, project management, and international development while contributing to a critical infrastructure project.

Join Our Team

Students interested in civil engineering, hydraulic design, structural analysis, and community development are invited to participate in this essential project that ensures water security for an entire community.
